Mishu — A story of two cities and one family

Our story begins in Antwerp, where the diamond trade is a language and the market a classroom. There, two generations of our family learned to read a rough stone like a musician reads a score — sensing the hidden note of brilliance and knowing where to cut so it will sing.

When markets shifted, we carried that knowledge to Mumbai. The move was more than geographic; it was a choice to combine Antwerp's exacting standards with India's extraordinary craftsmanship.

How a large stone becomes a story

01 Assessment

We begin with a careful reading of the rough: grain, inclusions, and potential

02 Planning

A bespoke cutting plan balances weight, brilliance, and market value

03 Execution

Sawing, bruting, and polishing by experienced hands with constant quality checks

04 Certification

Where appropriate, stones are submitted for GIA, HRD, or IGI grading

05 Presentation

Final stones are photographed, catalogued, and offered with full documentation

Why this matters

Large natural diamond stones are rare and complex. Thoughtful planning preserves value and reveals the stone's best light. Our process is designed to protect both beauty and investment.

The Atelier

Aiko Diamonds

Aiko Diamonds was born at the workbench where Mishu cutters and designers met to imagine what a great stone could become. What began as a conversation about a single exceptional polished diamond grew into an atelier that turns those stones into personal stories.

Each commission is treated as a quiet collaboration: we listen to the stone, learn the client's intent, and shape a design that lets the diamond speak.

Our process is intimate and deliberate. From the first private consultation to CAD previews and the final hand finish, Aiko Diamonds blends technical mastery with refined design so every piece becomes an heirloom.
